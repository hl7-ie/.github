## 🇮🇪 Welcome to HL7-IE

**HL7-IE** is an open-source community organization founded to develop, maintain, and test **FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources)** implementation guidance specifically tailored for Ireland. 

Our primary goal is to provide a collaborative space for developers, clinicians, and health informaticians to build **Proof of Concept (PoC)** recommendations that inform and accelerate the adoption of a National Implementation Guide (IG).

Crucially, this initiative is designed with a forward-looking approach, ensuring that Irish healthcare data flows seamlessly align with broader European standards, including the **European Health Data Space (EHDS)** and **XT-EHR (Cross-border eHealth)** requirements.

## 🎯 Our Mission

To accelerate the digital transformation of healthcare in Ireland by providing open, accessible, and robust FHIR implementation guidance that connects local systems with national and European networks.

## 🔑 Key Focus Areas

- **National FHIR IG Development:** Collaborating on baseline profiles, extensions, and value sets tailored to the Irish healthcare context.
- **Proof of Concept (PoC) Sandboxes:** Creating tangible, code-driven examples and reference implementations to validate IG recommendations before national rollout.
- **EHDS Alignment:** Ensuring all data models and implementation guides are fully compatible with the European Health Data Space guidelines.
- **XT-EHR Cross-Border Flow:** Supporting the technical interoperability needed for cross-border patient summaries, ePrescriptions, and lab results.

## 📂 Repository Structure

While our organization is growing, you can expect to find repositories categorized into:
- 📦 `ig-core`: The foundational draft Implementation Guides for Irish profiles.
- 🔬 `poc-examples`: Synthetic data and reference applications demonstrating cross-border workflows.
- 📚 `terminology`: Mapping of local Irish coding systems (e.g., HIQA standards, HSE coding) to SNOMED-CT, LOINC, and FHIR standards.
- 🛠️ `tooling`: Scripts, validators, and utilities to aid developers adopting HL7-IE standards.

## 🤝 How to Contribute

This is an open, community-driven effort! We welcome contributions from everyone—whether you are a seasoned FHIR expert, a clinical data modeler, or a developer new to healthcare interoperability.

1. **Join the Conversation:** Open an Issue in our repositories to discuss ideas, flag challenges, or propose new PoCs.
2. **Submit Code:** Fork a repository, make your changes, and submit a Pull Request.
3. **Review IGs:** Provide clinical or technical feedback on draft profiles.

*Please ensure you read our `CONTRIBUTING.md` and `CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md` before participating.*

## ⚖️ Disclaimer

**HL7-IE** is an open-source community initiative. While we are deeply committed to the HL7 FHIR standard and collaborate to support national stakeholders, this organization serves as a sandbox and community incubator for Proof of Concepts. Official national mandates will be published by the relevant statutory bodies (e.g., HSE, HIQA, Dept of Health).
